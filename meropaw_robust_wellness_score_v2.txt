
KASPER PETIFY – ROBUST WELLNESS SCORE (V2)
=========================================

This document defines a robust, explainable, rules-based Wellness Score
designed for consumer pet health tracking.

GOALS
-----
- Keep existing UI and score weights exactly the same:
  * Preventive Care: 0–50
  * Medical History: 0–30
  * Weight & Check-ins: 0–20
  * Total: 0–100
- Reduce false positives and noisy score changes
- Be trend-aware rather than single-point sensitive
- Remain fully explainable (no black-box ML)
- Be safe, conservative, and trust-preserving

WHAT THE WELLNESS SCORE REPRESENTS
----------------------------------
This score is a Wellness & Care Risk Index, not a diagnosis.

It summarizes:
- Preventive care adherence
- Stability of known medical history
- Recent weight trends and monitoring consistency

It is designed to answer:
“How stable and well-managed is this pet’s health right now, based on
available data?”

------------------------------------------------------------
HELPER FUNCTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

clamp(n, min, max)
- Ensures all scores stay within bounds

daysBetween(dateA, dateB)
- Computes difference in days, avoids month-rounding cliffs

median(array)
- Used for robust baseline calculations (outlier-resistant)

normalizePercent(value)
- Normalizes medication compliance whether stored as:
  * 0–1 (fraction)
  * 0–100 (percentage)

------------------------------------------------------------
1) PREVENTIVE CARE SCORE (0–50)
------------------------------------------------------------

What it measures:
- Whether core preventive actions are up to date

Inputs:
- vaccinesUpToDate (boolean)
- parasiteControlCurrent (boolean)
Optional future inputs:
- vaccinesDueDate (ISO date)
- parasiteControlDueDate (ISO date)

Scoring logic:
- Vaccines: up to 35 points
- Parasite control: up to 15 points

V2 improvements:
- If only booleans exist → behaves exactly like v1
- If due dates exist → overdue items decay smoothly instead of
  dropping to zero immediately

Why this is robust:
- No sudden score cliffs
- Future-proof for time-based prevention tracking

------------------------------------------------------------
2) MEDICAL HISTORY SCORE (0–30)
------------------------------------------------------------

What it measures:
- Baseline medical risk and stability

Inputs:
- chronicConditionsCount
- medicationCompliance (0–1 OR 0–100)
- recentSymptomsLogged (count in last 30 days)

Scoring logic:
Start at 30 points, subtract penalties:

A) Chronic conditions
- 5 points per condition
- Capped at 15 points max

B) Medication compliance
- Normalized to percentage
- Penalty = (100 - compliance) / 10
- Capped at 10 points

C) Symptoms
- First symptom is free (encourages logging)
- Each additional symptom subtracts 1 point
- Capped at 5 points

Why this is robust:
- Fixes unit ambiguity bug (0–1 vs 0–100)
- Does not punish responsible symptom logging
- Penalties are smooth and bounded

------------------------------------------------------------
3) WEIGHT & CHECK-INS SCORE (0–20)
------------------------------------------------------------

What it measures:
- Monitoring consistency
- Weight stability over time

Inputs:
- Weight history: [{ weight, date, source }]

V2 scoring uses TWO subcomponents:

A) Recency score (0–10)
- Based on days since last recorded weight
- Smooth linear decay
- No rounding cliffs

B) Stability score (0–10)
- Latest weight compared to a ROBUST BASELINE
- Baseline = median of recent historical weights
- Uses multiple points, not just the last two

Stability tiers:
- <3% change → 10 points
- <6% → 8 points
- <10% → 5 points
- <15% → 2 points
- ≥15% → 0 points

Why this is robust:
- Resistant to single bad weigh-ins
- Resistant to long gaps between measurements
- Encourages regular tracking without panic

------------------------------------------------------------
4) TOTAL WELLNESS SCORE (0–100)
------------------------------------------------------------

Total = Preventive (0–50)
      + Medical (0–30)
      + Weight (0–20)

Properties:
- Fully explainable
- Stable under small data changes
- Sensitive to trends, not noise

------------------------------------------------------------
WEIGHT ALERTS – ROBUST V2 LOGIC
------------------------------------------------------------

Problem with v1:
- Alerts triggered by only two weights
- No time-window requirement
- High false-positive rate

V2 alert requirements:
1) At least 3 total weight entries
2) Latest weight is recent (≤14 days old)
3) Baseline exists (median of last ~60 days)
4) Percent change exceeds threshold (default 5%)
5) Change is confirmed by a second recent measurement
   OR user is prompted to re-weigh

Alert decision outcomes:
- Alert sent: confirmed deviation from baseline
- No alert: insufficient data, old data, or noise
- Optional soft message: “Re-weigh to confirm change”

Why this is robust:
- Eliminates alerts caused by:
  * long historical gaps
  * one-off bad readings
  * format/source inconsistencies
